DESCRIPTION :

Ahmedabad is a major city in the western state of Gujarat, India. Known as the "Manchester of India" due to its historic textile industry, it is a vibrant cultural, economic, and educational hub. The city sits on the banks of the Sabarmati River, and is famous for its rich history, architectural heritage, and lively markets.

Key highlights about Ahmedabad:

Historical Significance: Founded by Sultan Ahmed Shah in the 15th century, Ahmedabad has a deep historical and cultural legacy. It played an important role in India's independence movement, particularly under the leadership of Mahatma Gandhi, who established the Sabarmati Ashram here.
